USE CASE NARRATIVE

Order Management (Divvy Stock Among Store Orders)

Use Case ID OM-05 Version 1.0
Use Case Name Divvy Stock Among Store Orders Date 2024-11-05
Author Tony Power Complexity High
Priority Medium
Use Case Type:
☒ Business Requirement
☐ System Analysis
Source Business Requirement
Primary Actor Warehouse
Other Actor(s) Store Manager
Stakeholders
  • Warehouse Operations
  • Store Managers
Description This use case describes how the warehouse allocates limited stock among multiple pending store orders. When stock levels are insufficient to fully meet all store requests, available inventory is divided equitably based on defined rules or priorities.
Pre Condition(s) Multiple store orders with overlapping inventory requests exist in the system.
The warehouse has limited stock of requested items, necessitating equitable distribution among stores.
Triggers An item is available in limited quantities and must be divided among multiple pending orders.

Basic Flow (Typical Course of Events)

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
Warehouse selects “Divvy Stock Among Orders” option for an item System lists all active orders requesting the selected item
Actor reviews pending orders and prioritization criteria (e.g., order date, store needs) System displays order details, quantity requested, and item availability
Actor assigns stock quantities to each order based on availability and priorities System updates each store’s order with allocated quantities, adjusting for backorders if needed
Actor confirms stock distribution System finalizes the updated orders, sending them to fulfillment as appropriate

Alternate Flow

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
If no inventory is available for an item System notifies actor that all affected orders will be backordered

Conclusion

The stock is allocated among store orders based on availability, priority, and order specifics.

Post Condition(s)

Orders are updated to reflect the allocated quantities, and any unfulfilled quantities are marked for backorder.

Additional Information

Business Rules
  • Orders with older submission dates or specific priority flags are fulfilled first when dividing stock.
  • Partial quantities allocated to orders must be noted clearly to inform fulfillment and the store.
Special Requirements
  • System must alert stores when their order quantities are adjusted, providing transparency on partial fulfillment.
Constraints and Specifications Divvying stock must comply with any store-specific needs or stock minimums.
Technology and Data Real-time inventory tracking ensures accurate allocation and minimizes errors in stock availability.
Frequency Stock division occurs regularly when demand exceeds supply for certain items.
Assumptions Inventory is updated and verified before proceeding with stock allocation.
Notes / Open Issues Review any store-specific prioritization to refine equitable distribution when supply is low.
