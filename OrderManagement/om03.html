Order Management (Receive Store Order)

Author Tony Power Complexity Medium
Priority High

Source Business Requirement
Primary Actor Warehouse Worker, Store Manager
Other Actor(s) Delivery Driver, Store
Stakeholders
  • Inventory Management
  • Store Operations
Description This use case describes the process of receiving a store order. When an order arrives from the warehouse or delivery driver, it is checked for accuracy and logged into the inventory system to update stock levels.
Pre Condition(s) A store order has been placed and dispatched from the warehouse.
Warehouse Worker, Store Manager, or Store Employee is authorized to receive orders.
Triggers The arrival of a store order at the designated location.

Basic Flow (Typical Course of Events)

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
Warehouse Worker or Store Manager selects the “Receive Store Order” option System displays a list of expected incoming store orders for the day
Actor identifies the order to be received System loads details for the selected order, including items and quantities
Actor checks items against the delivery manifest System verifies the quantities and matches items against the order
Actor confirms the order is correct System logs the order as “Received,” updates inventory, and issues a receipt
Actor submits any discrepancies found System flags the order for further review and records the issue

Alternate Flow

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
Actor finds missing or damaged items during the check System prompts actor to record details of discrepancies, such as missing or damaged items

Conclusion

The store order is marked as received, inventory is updated, and any discrepancies are recorded for follow-up.

Post Condition(s)

Inventory levels are updated in the store, and the order status is logged as “Received” in the system.

Additional Information

Business Rules
  • Orders must be checked in full and signed off by an authorized actor.
  • Discrepancies require an additional review by the warehouse team or manager.
Special Requirements
  • System should support barcode scanning for quick item checks.
  • Real-time inventory updates should be reflected as soon as the order is marked as received.
Constraints and Specifications All discrepancies must be resolved within a certain timeframe to maintain inventory accuracy.
Technology and Data Order data is managed via the inventory system, with real-time updates.
Frequency Store orders are typically received weekly or as per the inventory schedule.
Assumptions Delivery information is accurate and up-to-date in the system.
Notes / Open Issues Consider adding automated discrepancy alerts to notify the warehouse manager for quicker resolution.
