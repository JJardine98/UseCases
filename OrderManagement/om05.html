Order Management (Process Emergency Order)

Use Case ID OM-05 Version 1.0
Use Case Name Process Emergency Order Date 2024-11-05
Author Tony Power Complexity High
Priority High
Use Case Type:
☒ Business Requirement
☐ System Analysis
Source Business Requirement
Primary Actor Warehouse Worker
Other Actor(s) Store Manager
Stakeholders
  • Store Operations
  • Warehouse Management
Description This use case outlines the process for handling emergency orders in the warehouse, prioritizing their preparation and allocation. The Warehouse Worker must assess item availability, assign quantities promptly, and expedite fulfillment.
Pre Condition(s) An emergency order has been submitted by a store and appears in the warehouse’s system for urgent processing.
The Warehouse Worker has permissions to process and prioritize emergency orders.
Triggers Notification of a new emergency order requiring immediate action.

Basic Flow (Typical Course of Events)

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
Warehouse Worker selects “Process Emergency Order” option System displays the emergency order flagged for priority
Actor verifies items in the emergency order System shows requested items with availability and flags shortages
Actor assigns available quantities to fulfill the order System updates quantities allocated to the emergency order, with preference over regular orders
Actor confirms order completion System marks the emergency order as “Processed” and sends it to fulfillment for expedited handling

Alternate Flow

Actor Action System/Result, Info required
If items are unavailable System prompts actor to create or update a backorder, ensuring the order will be fulfilled upon restock

Conclusion

The emergency order is processed and prioritized for rapid fulfillment.

Post Condition(s)

The emergency order is sent for immediate fulfillment, and any unfulfilled items are backordered.

Additional Information

Business Rules
  • Emergency orders take precedence over standard orders in both processing and fulfillment.
  • Items on emergency orders that are unavailable are flagged and prioritized once restocked.
Special Requirements
  • The system should support notifications to update the requesting store on the status of their emergency order.
  • Emergency orders should reflect high-priority status in the inventory and fulfillment queues.
Constraints and Specifications Emergency order processing must align with warehouse capacity and operational limits.
Technology and Data Real-time updates on inventory levels are essential to expedite processing of emergency orders.
Frequency Emergency orders are occasional but must be processed immediately as they appear.
Assumptions Inventory data is accurate and up-to-date to avoid processing delays.
Notes / Open Issues Explore automated restocking alerts to further reduce emergency order dependency.
